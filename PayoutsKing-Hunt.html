1. Executive Summary

PayoutsKing is an active ransomware and extortion operation observed from mid-2025 into 2026. Public reporting connects the group to pragmatic ransomware tradecraft: credential abuse, exposed remote access, social engineering through Microsoft Teams and Quick Assist, data theft, security control interference, backup deletion, and ransomware encryption.

The highest-value defensive angle is not a single hash or leak-site artifact. The most actionable hunting coverage is behavior: unauthorized QEMU execution as SYSTEM with SSH port forwarding, suspicious virtual disk files, credential database staging, remote support tooling appearing after social engineering, Rclone or SFTP exfiltration, and ransomware preparation actions such as shadow copy deletion and event log clearing.

Defender priority: Baseline and hunt for QEMU, ScreenConnect, Quick Assist, Rclone, shadow-copy operations, and AD credential file staging across servers, admin workstations, and VPN-exposed environments.

8. CrowdStrike LogScale CQL Hunt Queries

Field names are schema-dependent. Validate `#event_simpleName`, `ImageFileName`, `CommandLine`, `FileName`, `TargetFileName`, `RemoteAddressIP4`, and user/host fields against your Falcon tenant before promoting detections.

Q1. QEMU Launch With SSH Port Forwarding or Suspicious Disk

CONF: high | FP: medium | COST: low-medium | Requires: process command line

Looks for: qemu-system-x86_64.exe launched with SSH forwarding, odd VM disk names, or STAC4713 port values. Accomplishes: catches covert VM-based access before the attacker hides tooling and tunnels inside the guest OS.

#event_simpleName=/ProcessRollup2|SyntheticProcessRollup2/i
| ImageFileName=/\\qemu-system-x86_64\.exe$/i
| CommandLine=/(hostfwd|guestfwd|redir|:22|32567|22022|\.qcow2|vault\.db|bisrv\.dll|custom\.qcow2)/i
| table([@timestamp, ComputerName, UserName, ParentBaseFileName, ImageFileName, CommandLine, SHA256HashData])

Q2. TPMProfiler Scheduled Task or SYSTEM QEMU Persistence

CONF: high | FP: low-medium | COST: low | Requires: process and scheduled task telemetry

Looks for: the reported TPMProfiler scheduled task, QEMU launch strings, and suspicious disk names. Accomplishes: identifies persistence used to relaunch hidden virtualization under privileged context.

#event_simpleName=/ProcessRollup2|ScheduledTaskRegistered|ScheduledTaskModified/i
| (CommandLine=/TPMProfiler|qemu-system-x86_64|vault\.db|bisrv\.dll/i OR FileName=/TPMProfiler/i)
| table([@timestamp, ComputerName, UserName, EventType, ParentBaseFileName, ImageFileName, CommandLine, FileName])

Q3. New Remote Support or RMM Tool After User Interaction

CONF: medium | FP: medium-high | COST: medium | Requires: process, service, file, optional identity logs

Looks for: Quick Assist, ScreenConnect, suspicious relay references, and service names tied to reported campaigns. Accomplishes: helps detect social-engineering-to-remote-control handoff before hands-on-keyboard activity accelerates.

#event_simpleName=/ProcessRollup2|ServiceStarted|ServiceInstalled|PeFileWritten/i
| (ImageFileName=/\\quickassist\.exe$|\\ScreenConnect\.ClientService\.exe$/i OR CommandLine=/QuickAssist|ScreenConnect|CtxAppVCOMService|AppMgmt|vtps\.us/i OR FileName=/ScreenConnect|QuickAssist/i)
| table([@timestamp, ComputerName, UserName, EventType, ParentBaseFileName, ImageFileName, CommandLine, FileName, TargetFileName])

Q4. AD Credential Database and Registry Hive Staging

CONF: high | FP: low-medium | COST: medium | Requires: process and file write telemetry on DCs

Looks for: NTDS.dit, SAM, and SYSTEM hive access or copies, including VSS-assisted staging. Accomplishes: detects credential theft preparation that often precedes privilege escalation, lateral movement, and broad encryption.

#event_simpleName=/ProcessRollup2|PeFileWritten|FileCreateInfo/i
| (CommandLine=/ntds\.dit|\\SAM\b|\\SYSTEM\b|vssuirun|shadowcopy|copy .*\\\\.*\\admin\$|print /i OR TargetFileName=/\\ntds\.dit$|\\SAM$|\\SYSTEM$/i)
| table([@timestamp, ComputerName, UserName, EventType, ParentBaseFileName, ImageFileName, CommandLine, TargetFileName])

Q5. Rclone, SFTP, FTP, or SSH Exfiltration Tooling

CONF: medium-high | FP: medium | COST: medium | Requires: process and network telemetry

Looks for: Rclone and transfer utilities using SSH, SFTP, FTP, unusual ports, or sync/copy syntax. Accomplishes: spots data staging and outbound movement that can drive the double-extortion phase.

#event_simpleName=/ProcessRollup2|NetworkConnectIP4/i
| (ImageFileName=/\\rclone\.exe$|\\ssh\.exe$|\\sftp\.exe$|\\ftp\.exe$/i OR CommandLine=/rclone|sftp|ftp |--config|--transfers|--bwlimit|copy .*:|sync .*:/i OR RemotePort=/22|2222|22022|32567/)
| table([@timestamp, ComputerName, UserName, EventType, ImageFileName, CommandLine, RemoteAddressIP4, RemotePort])

Q6. Ransomware Preparation: Shadow Copies, Event Logs, and Ransom Note

CONF: high | FP: medium | COST: low | Requires: process, file, and Windows event telemetry

Looks for: shadow copy deletion, recovery tampering, event log clearing, and the reported ransom note name. Accomplishes: gives responders a late-stage tripwire for active ransomware preparation and evidence destruction.

#event_simpleName=/ProcessRollup2|FileCreateInfo|EventLogCleared/i
| (CommandLine=/vssadmin.*delete shadows|wmic.*shadowcopy.*delete|wevtutil.*cl|Clear-EventLog|bcdedit.*recoveryenabled|readme_locker\.txt/i OR TargetFileName=/\\readme_locker\.txt$/i)
| table([@timestamp, ComputerName, UserName, EventType, ParentBaseFileName, ImageFileName, CommandLine, TargetFileName])

12. Hardening - Tiered & Deployable

Immediate

  • Audit and restrict exposed SonicWall, Cisco SSL VPN, NetScaler, and SolarWinds Web Help Desk surfaces; verify MFA and patch status.
  • Block or approval-gate unsanctioned QEMU, Rclone, ScreenConnect, and remote support execution on servers.
  • Alert on shadow copy deletion and event log clearing from non-backup tooling.

Near Term

  • Deploy application control for virtualization and RMM utilities by host role.
  • Centralize VPN, Teams, Quick Assist, and endpoint telemetry for social-engineering correlation.
  • Protect domain controllers with enhanced file access auditing for NTDS.dit, SAM, and SYSTEM.

Strategic

  • Segment hypervisor management and restrict admin paths to hardened workstations.
  • Implement recovery testing for immutable backups and out-of-band identity recovery.
  • Continuously validate ransomware controls through safe attack emulation.

13. Deployable Playbooks

Rapid QEMU Triage

Scope: SOC / endpoint owner / identity owner
1. Identify host, user, parent process, command line, signer, and install path.
2. Check for TPMProfiler, qemu-system-x86_64.exe, vault.db, bisrv.dll, custom.qcow2, port forwarding, and outbound SSH.
3. Isolate if QEMU is unauthorized on a server, DC, admin workstation, or VPN-adjacent host.
4. Pull process tree, scheduled tasks, services, persistence keys, file artifacts, and network connections.
5. Review domain controller and privileged account activity within 72 hours before first QEMU execution.
